History

Early history

Two childhood friends and Malaysians, Hafiz Azman and Winston Lee, decided to make a little Flash game in the summer of 2011 while studying in university in the UK. It was their first time doing game development, and it quickly became an addictive past-time; every subsequent summer and winter vacation from that point on was spent on this game, all the way till graduation. During the Christmas holidays of 2012 they finally released an early demo on TIGSource in a thread called 'Rhythm Doctor - a tough-as-nails rhythm game!'. To their surprise it was well-received! On a whim, they decided to enter it for the IGFs the following year. To their (bigger) surprise they were selected! That was the year they set foot on US soil for the first time.

At GDC, they met Giacomo Preciado, a programmer from Peru, and he joined as 7th Beat Games' third member. Today after graduation, the three work on the game from their respective home countries.

You can find more details about the game's inception in this Gamasutra interview: Road to the Student IGF.
